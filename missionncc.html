01Market opportunity

A captive audience, an annual exam cycle, and no modern competition.

The National Cadet Corps is the world's largest uniformed youth organisation. Defence Minister Rajnath Singh has approved expansion to 20 lakh (2 million) cadets, up from the current ~17 lakh. Every year, roughly 1.5–2 lakh cadets sit for the A, B, and C certificate exams held in January–February. Yet there is no single app purpose-built for them.

Why this is underserved

The NCC prep market today is fragmented across scanned PDFs, handwritten notes shared on WhatsApp, unstructured YouTube playlists, and paper booklets sold at ₹100–150 per paper. There is no Duolingo-style structured learning, no progress tracking, and no way for a cadet in a rural town to access the same quality of preparation as one in Delhi. A well-designed app captures this entire market by default — the bar is low, and the audience is captive.

The wedge: Win NCC first (small, focused, low competition). Then expand outward to NDA, CDS, AFCAT, and SSB — where the same defence-aspirant persona pays significantly more and competition is fierce but fragmented. NCC becomes the top of the funnel.

02The problem today

What cadets actually do when the exam date approaches.

1Scattered PDFs
Cadets rely on WhatsApp-forwarded scanned PDFs of unknown vintage. Questions, options, and answers are often written by hand in the margins. No way to know if content is current.
2No structure
No syllabus mapping, no chapter progression, no way to know what's been covered or what's weak. Cadets open a PDF and guess where to start.
3Paper pricing
Competitor apps like Tejas NCC Academy charge ~₹150 per paper. For a student from a rural area, that adds up fast across 4+ subject papers.
4Zero feedback
Reading a PDF gives no feedback. There's no "here's what you got wrong and here's what to do about it" — which is exactly what a timed, negative-marked exam punishes.
5Drill practice is manual
Drill and practical training is taught only in parades and camps. No one revisits it between sessions. Muscle memory fades, and procedural knowledge drops out of working memory.
6Rural friction
Limited bandwidth, entry-level Android phones, irregular data packs. Most "edtech" apps assume a metro user with an iPhone and unlimited Jio. This audience doesn't.
"Every phone has something like Duolingo now. Points, streaks, progress shown plainly. Why doesn't NCC have that?" — field note from a cadet workshop (200 participants)

03Who we're building for

The primary user — and what they look like on a Wednesday evening.

Primary · The exam-bound cadet

Age 15–21. School (Class 9–12) or first-year college. Lives in a Tier 2/3 town or rural district. Parade time is fixed; exam is the bottleneck. Owns an entry-level Android (4–6 GB RAM). Data is metered. Watches YouTube in Hindi or their mother tongue. Willing to pay ₹99–499 for something that feels valuable. Not willing to pay ₹1,000+ for a course.

Secondary · The ANO & instructor

Associate NCC Officer, PI staff, or school teacher doubling as NCC in-charge. Wants their cadets to perform well — their unit's reputation depends on exam pass rates. Will recommend the app if it demonstrably helps. May adopt institutional plans if priced for a full unit (50–200 cadets).

Jobs to be done

When a cadet picks up this app, they are hiring it to do one of three things: (a) make them pass the certificate exam with a good grade, (b) help them retain drill and subject knowledge between parades, (c) build a foundation that carries into NDA/CDS prep later. The MVP has to deliver on (a) cleanly. Everything else is a bonus.

04Product positioning

The one-line pitch.

Positioning statement: Cadet Drill is a mobile-first preparation platform for NCC cadets that replaces scattered PDFs with structured lessons, timed mock tests, and practical drill guides — at a price any cadet can afford.

What it is, what it isn't

It is

A focused, mobile-native, offline-capable prep tool. Short lessons, adaptive quizzes, honest progress tracking. Works on a ₹8,000 Android with patchy 4G. Feels like training, not reading.

It isn't

A video-lecture library. A social network for cadets. A replacement for the parade ground. A bloated course platform. An AI chatbot wrapper. Every feature that doesn't help a cadet pass their exam gets deferred to v2 or killed.

05MVP scope (6 weeks)

The smallest product that demonstrably helps a cadet pass the exam.

The MVP should be small enough to build in six weeks with one developer plus Claude Code, and sharp enough that 100 cadets in a pilot say "this is useful — when is the next release?" Scope discipline is the most important decision at this stage. The list below is what ships in v1. Everything else waits.

What ships in v1

1Onboarding & goal set
Pick wing (Army / Navy / Air). Pick target certificate (A / B / C). Get a tailored study path. No signup wall — phone OTP only.
2Structured syllabus
Subjects → chapters → short (3–5 min) lessons. Each lesson ends with a 5-question check-for-understanding quiz. Skip-forward not allowed until the quiz is passed.
3MCQ bank (2,000+ Qs)
Tagged by subject, chapter, difficulty, certificate level. Explanations on every question. This is the heart of the product — the thing most cadets will spend the most time in.
4Timed mock tests
Full-length mock papers with negative marking (−0.25 per wrong answer, matching the real exam). Post-test analytics: weak topics, time-per-question, percentile vs peers.
5Drill video guides
Short (30–90s) clips for each drill movement, with a step-by-step checklist cadets can tick off while practising. No fancy AI posture detection yet — just good content, well indexed.
6Progress & streaks
XP, day streak, rank badges (Cadet → Lance Corporal → Corporal → Sergeant). Weekly summary: "You mastered 12 topics this week." The dopamine layer that keeps cadets opening the app.
7Offline mode
Lessons, quizzes, and mocks downloadable for offline use. Progress syncs when the cadet comes back online. Non-negotiable — this is what makes the app viable in rural districts.
8Bilingual from day one
English + Hindi at launch. Add a third language (Tamil or Telugu) after pilot feedback confirms demand. All MCQs stored with translation fields, not just English.

Explicitly NOT in v1

Live classes. Community / chat / forums. AI tutor. Creator tools for teachers. Video lectures longer than 5 minutes. Web app (mobile-first; web comes in Phase 2). Referral program. Coupon engine. Scholarship tracking. All of these are worth building — just not before we know cadets open the app 4 times a week.

07Tech architecture

Boring, shippable, and scales to 10 lakh users without a rewrite.

LayerChoiceWhy
Mobile app Flutter One codebase → Android + iOS. Fast iteration. Good performance on entry-level devices. Large talent pool in India.
Backend (MVP) Supabase (Postgres + Auth + Storage) Zero devops for the first 6 months. Phone OTP auth built in. Row-level security. Switchable to self-hosted Postgres later without rewriting.
Content delivery Cloudflare R2 + CDN Video and image assets served cheaply. Zero egress fees matter a lot when traffic scales.
Offline cache Drift (SQLite for Flutter) Type-safe, schema-migrated local DB. Sync resolver runs on login and on manual pull.
Analytics PostHog (self-hostable) + Supabase events Product analytics without sending cadet data to ad networks. Funnels and retention cohorts out of the box.
Payments Razorpay + UPI Autopay UPI is the only payment method that matters for this audience. Autopay for subscriptions. No card friction.
Push / reminders Firebase Cloud Messaging Free, reliable, works on every Android. Used for streak reminders and exam countdown.
AI layer (later) Claude API for adaptive quiz generation & explanation drafts Not in MVP. In v2, use Claude to generate explanation drafts for MCQs and to build adaptive-difficulty practice sessions.
Build approach: Use Claude Code for the full stack — scaffolding Flutter, writing Supabase migrations, generating MCQ seed data from the existing PDF bank, writing tests. A single developer with Claude Code should ship the MVP in 6 focused weeks.

Pricing logic

At ₹99/month, a cadet pays less than a single paper PDF from Tejas (~₹150) — and gets an entire app with unlimited practice. The ₹499 seasonal bundle is the tier most cadets will actually buy: it covers the full exam run-up from August to February. The ₹5,000/unit institutional tier is a high-margin, low-volume upsell that lets us sell to ANOs and principals for ~₹50/cadet — an easier budget conversation than 100 individual ₹499 payments.

09Go-to-market

Where the first 10,000 users come from.

1Workshop pilot
Weeks 1–6 post-MVP. Run the Cadet Drill app at Pavan's existing 200-cadet workshops. Onboard every attendee. Watch what they do. Fix everything that breaks. Zero paid acquisition — this is product-discovery disguised as distribution.
2ANO & instructor outreach
Warm intros through the UP directorate and existing instructor networks. Send each ANO a demo link, a one-pager, and an offer to onboard their full unit free for 60 days. A single enthusiastic ANO can bring 100+ cadets.
3WhatsApp & Telegram seeding
Where cadets already are. Join the 50+ existing NCC prep Telegram groups. Don't spam — share genuinely useful free content (a "practice 10 MCQs a day" bot, a drill checklist PDF) with a soft link to the app.
4YouTube & short-form
A channel that answers the top 50 search queries ("NCC B certificate syllabus 2026", "drill movements in Hindi", etc.). One video a week, sub-5 minutes, optimised for the Indian YouTube algorithm. App link in every description.
5Exam-season campaigns
Paid ads turn on only from November to February — the three months before the main exam. Meta + YouTube Shorts, ₹20–50 CPL target. Rest of year stays organic.
6Referral loop
v2. Every free-tier cadet gets a shareable unit code. Any friend who signs up with that code gives both cadets a month of Pro free. Kicks in only after we know retention is solid.

11Risks & mitigations

What could kill this, and what we'll do about it.

RiskLikelihoodMitigation
Content accuracy — MCQs or lesson facts are wrong, cadets fail because of us Medium Every question and lesson reviewed by at least one serving/retired ANO before publishing. User-reportable errors with a 48h SLA to fix.
Syllabus revision — NCC changes its syllabus and our content becomes stale overnight Medium Build content as versioned, tagged data (not baked into the app). Every piece of content has a "valid for [exam year]" field. Hot updates without app releases.
Willingness to pay — cadets from rural/low-income backgrounds won't convert to paid Medium-High Aggressive free tier (genuinely useful). Seasonal ₹499 bundle priced below one paper PDF. Unit plans sold to ANOs so the cadet doesn't pay directly.
Someone big enters the space — Unacademy, BYJU'S, PhysicsWallah launch an NCC vertical Low-Medium Move fast. Win the first 50k cadets before anyone notices. Deep NCC-specific features (drill videos, unit dashboards) are hard to copy without domain expertise.
Content generation effort — seeding 2,000 MCQs from scratch is expensive High Use Claude to generate MCQ drafts from existing PDFs/handbooks, reviewed by a subject-matter editor. 10× faster than writing from scratch.
Payment failures on entry-level phones — UPI Autopay setup fails on older Android Low Offer one-time ₹499 seasonal pass as the primary purchase flow; subscription is secondary. Fewer moving parts = fewer failure modes.

North Star metric

The single number the team watches weekly: "Weekly Active Cadets who completed at least one timed mock." This captures genuine value — not just opens or lesson views, but the behaviour most correlated with exam success. If this number is growing, the rest of the metrics will follow.
