Map Reading

⬇️
Chapter 3 8 min read

Conventional Signs & Symbols

Conventional signs are standardised symbols used on topographical maps to represent physical and cultural features on the ground. Learning these symbols is essential for reading any military map.

A topographical map uses a scale of 1:50,000, meaning 1 cm on the map equals 500 metres on the ground.

Categories of Conventional Signs

  • Natural features: Rivers, hills, forests, swamps
  • Man-made features: Roads, railways, buildings, bridges
  • Administrative: District boundaries, state lines
  • Vegetation: Cultivated land, scrub, jungle

Relief Representation

Contour lines join points of equal elevation. The vertical distance between two contour lines is called the Contour Interval (CI). On a 1:50,000 map, the CI is typically 20 metres.

Rule: Closely spaced contour lines = steep slope. Widely spaced = gentle slope.

Index contours (every 5th line) are drawn thicker and labelled with their elevation value.

Question 7 of 10 · Map Reading
On a 1:50,000 topographical map, the contour interval is 20 metres. If a hill has 8 contour lines between its base and summit, what is the approximate height difference?
A
100 metres
B
120 metres
C
160 metres
D
200 metres
Explanation

Height difference = Number of contour intervals × CI = 8 × 20 = 160 metres. Count intervals between lines, not the lines themselves.
